Key Concepts Summary

After completing the CoE course, you should understand these concepts

1. Tokenization: How AI Reads Text

AI can't read words like you do. It converts text into numbers called "tokens" ‚Äî puzzle pieces the AI uses to understand language.

Key Insight What It Means
Spaces are tokens too " my" is different from "my"
Case matters "Hello" and "hello" are different tokens
Rare words get split "austin" ‚Üí "aust" + "in"
Why This Matters

AI doesn't "understand" words ‚Äî it processes token IDs. This is why it can struggle with spelling and rare names.

2. Context Window: AI's Memory

The context window is AI's short-term memory ‚Äî how much text it can "see" at once. Everything outside the window is forgotten.

Model Context Window Approximate Words
GPT-3.5 ~4,000 tokens ~3,000 words
GPT-4 ~128,000 tokens ~100,000 words
Claude 3 ~200,000 tokens ~150,000 words
Practical Implication

If a conversation is too long, the AI might "forget" important details from the beginning. Claude's large context window helps, but very long conversations may still lose early context.

3. How AI Generates Responses (Prediction)

AI generates text one token at a time by predicting "what word is most likely to come next?" It's like autocomplete on your phone, but much more sophisticated.

Temperature Setting Effect Best For
Low (0.0-0.3) Predictable, consistent responses Factual answers, code, structured data
High (0.7-1.0) Creative, varied responses Brainstorming, creative writing

4. Attention: How AI Understands Context

The breakthrough that made modern AI possible. When processing a word, the AI "looks at" all other words to understand meaning.

Example

In "I need help tracking my order" ‚Äî the word "order" pays attention to "tracking" to understand the context.

This is how AI knows "bank" means different things in "river bank" vs "bank account" ‚Äî by looking at surrounding words.

5. Hallucinations: Why AI Gets Things Wrong

AI doesn't "know" facts ‚Äî it predicts statistically likely text. When it confidently states something false, it's because that pattern was statistically common in its training data.

Key Limitation to Understand

LLMs don't truly "understand" in the human sense ‚Äî they predict likely next words based on patterns. This is why they can sometimes produce confident-sounding but incorrect information (hallucinations).

Implication: This is why grounding AI in real-time data (like customer databases or web search) is so important.

Agents vs. Chatbots vs. Automation

Understanding the difference helps you see where AI is headed

Type What It Does Example
Chatbot Answers questions based on training data Customer support FAQ bot
Automation Follows predefined rules without reasoning Zapier workflow, email rules
AI Agent Reasons, uses tools, and takes actions autonomously PMM Agent analyzing Slack
The Agent Loop

Think ‚Üí Act ‚Üí Observe ‚Üí (Repeat until done)

Agents don't just respond ‚Äî they reason about what to do next, take action, observe the result, and iterate. This is fundamentally different from chatbots.

Gladly's 9-agent initiative builds on these concepts ‚Äî understanding the difference between chatbots and agents helps you see where AI is headed. The agents being built use all these principles: context windows, attention, and the Think ‚Üí Act ‚Üí Observe loop.
