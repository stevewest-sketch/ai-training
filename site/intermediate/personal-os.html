Step 4: Understanding System Connections

Your Personal OS pulls from four connected systems. Here's how each one works:

System What Claude Searches Why It Matters
Slack Messages to you, from manager, from direct reports, priority channels Surfaces urgent items and team activity
Gmail Gemini meeting notes, Doc comments/mentions Captures action items and requests
Calendar Today's meetings Enables meeting prep and context
Drive Recent document updates Tracks changes to key files
Gemini Meeting Notes

Google Meet automatically generates meeting summaries via Gemini. These are emailed to you with subject "Notes by Gemini" and include action items, decisions, key points, and follow-ups. Claude searches for these automatically during your briefing.

Step 6: Customize and Iterate

No setup is perfect on the first try. Here's how to refine your Personal OS based on what you see:

If You Notice... Then Adjust...
Briefing is too long Move sections to "over to you" options
Missing meeting context Check that Gemini notes are in your Gmail
Wrong Slack channels Update Priority Slack Channels in CLAUDE.md
Too much noise Add "IGNORE" rules for newsletters, notifications
Missing team info Expand your team.md with more detail

Role-Specific Adjustments

Not all sections apply to everyone

For Individual Contributors (ICs):

  • Remove "Team OKR Status" and "Team Pulse" sections
  • Focus on your own deliverables and blockers
  • Add project-specific tracking

For Managers:

  • Keep all team-related sections
  • Add 1:1 prep emphasis
  • Include team Slack monitoring

For Customer-Facing Roles:

  • Add deal/account channel monitoring
  • Include customer health signals
  • Track renewal/expansion activity
